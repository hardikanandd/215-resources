11. Printing in the Sumwalt labs: Please limit the amount of stuff you print in the Sumwalt labs. It’s ok to print up the occasional short document, or web site you need, but if you need to do any serious printing (long papers, term papers, etc.) you need to print these in the labs in Swearingen and use your 500 page print quota there. Although there is no electronically enforced print quota in the Sumwalt labs, printer use is monitored. An easy rule of thumb is to keep your printing in Sumwalt under 200 pages per semester.


12. Preparation: It is important that you prepare for your lab or lectures in advance. This means having read and familiarized yourself will all the relevant material and having tested it out to make sure it works as you expect it to in the labs. You need to actually test what you plan on presenting in the labs. Something you’ve tested at home on your computer (where you most likely have administrator privileges) and in the labs (where you are simply a high-class user) can yield completely different results. This is further complicated by the fact that the Sumwalt labs are on a mostly self-contained network, with considerable security. Make sure you test in advance. There are few things as embarrassing for an instructor as to stand up and explain something and then have it not work.

14. Try and make the class enjoyable for the students: Try and make CSCE 101 and 102 a fun class to take. Even if you don’t actually enjoy the material, try and pretend like you do. Look for little extras and tricks you can show your students that will allow them to do “neat” things on the computer. You should try and avoid showing your students any overly complicated “tricks”, but something simple that has a dramatic visual effect is usually the best type. Try and get your students excited and keep them excited throughout the class. You will find that most students enjoy the web page creation (XHTML/HTML) aspects of the class the most. Try and use this to keep their energy up and to keep it up through some of the less enjoyable topics.


15. Troubleshooting in Lab:

- If a row of computers, or three side-by-side go dead, look for something unplugged.

- If you’re helping a student fix a file, and you can’t find any errors, make sure the file being edited and viewed is the same file, not a file with the same name in a different directory.

- Don’t cut and paste HTML/XHTML from Microsoft Word into notepad. This will change your quote marks into something that looks like quotes but that aren’t actually quotes. This is an error you may never notice.
